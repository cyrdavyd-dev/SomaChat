1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the mobile application Soma Chat (“the App”) processes information on your device. The App is designed for local health-related journaling in a chat-style interface (messages, vitals, symptoms, prescriptions, and related attachments). Some features are offered under a free trial and/or a paid subscription as described below.

2. Data we process

Depending on what you enter, the App may store on your device:

  • Profile data: display name, date of birth, height, weight, gender (as selected), blood type, country/region key, allergies, conditions, medications, and a local path to a profile photo if you choose one.
  • Chat content: message text, timestamps, message type, and attached files (copies kept in the App’s private data area).
  • App preferences: e.g. the identifier of the active chat thread; the UTC time of the first launch (to run the free trial); a cached snapshot of subscription status (such as whether access is active, renewal flag, expiry time, and product identifier); and an anonymous, per-install identifier generated on the device to link in-app purchases to this installation (for store / billing APIs).

Health and chat content are processed on your device. The App does not provide a separate user account or cloud sync of your health journal to the operator’s own servers.

3. Subscriptions, free trial, and in-app purchases

Free trial. The App includes a time-limited trial (by default 14 days from the first launch). Only the first-launch timestamp is stored locally to compute the trial; no payment is required to start the trial.

Paid subscription. Where available, paid access is purchased through Google Play (Android) or the Apple App Store (iOS / iPadOS / Mac, as applicable). Payment is processed by Google or Apple. The App’s operator does not receive your full payment card details.

What is shared for billing. To verify purchases and subscription status, the App communicates with the app store and, where integrated, a subscription helper SDK (the codebase is set up for providers such as RevenueCat alongside native store billing). That flow typically involves billing-related data only, such as subscription state, product identifiers, renewal and expiry information, and identifiers associated with your store account, as defined by Google and Apple (and by the billing SDK provider if used). It does not include the text of your health journal as part of standard purchase validation.

Premium features. After the trial ends, certain actions may require an active subscription, including (as implemented in the App): sending structured health-related messages and pain entries, using structured entries from the “+” menu, opening metric history, exporting the chat to PDF, and creating additional profiles beyond the first profile. Reading the chat, basic note-style use, and the first profile may remain available without premium, subject to the version you have installed.

Manage or cancel. You can manage or cancel a subscription in your Google Play or Apple ID subscription settings. The App may open or link to the platform’s subscription management page.

For how Google and Apple process data for purchases, see their respective privacy policies and store terms.

4. Where data is stored

Health and journal data are stored locally using a SQLite database and files under the application’s app data directory on your device. Temporary files may be created in the cache (for example when generating exports).

The App does not send your health or chat content to the operator’s servers for storage. Subscription validation uses the app store (and billing SDK if present), not the text of your journal entries. There is no in-app analytics or crash-reporting SDK in the project that sends your journal content to the developer.

5. Sharing and third parties

The operator does not receive your health journal from the App by default. Data may leave your device when you initiate an action, or through platform services, for example:

  • In-app purchases: Google Play and/or Apple process the transaction and related subscription data under their own policies.
  • Export / Share: PDF or related files via the operating system share sheet — you choose the receiving app (email, messenger, cloud storage, etc.).
  • Open attachment: opening a file in another app using OS features.
  • Device backup: depending on your device settings, application data may be included in system backups (including cloud backup where enabled).

6. Network and permissions

On Android, the manifest may declare Internet and network-state access, Google Play Billing (com.android.vending.BILLING), camera (optional hardware, for taking photos where the App supports it), and vibration. Network access is required for store billing and related validation, not for uploading your journal text to the operator.

Access to photos (gallery or camera, where offered) and files for attachments is requested through standard system pickers or capture flows when you add content.

7. Security and your responsibility

Protect your device (screen lock, trusted apps). The operator cannot guarantee the confidentiality of data if the device is compromised or if you share exported files with others.

8. Children

The App is not directed at collecting data from children. If a parent or guardian enters information about a child, they do so voluntarily and under their own responsibility.

9. Changes

This Policy may be updated if the App’s functionality changes (including subscription or trial rules). The current version will be published at the same URL you use for the store listing.
